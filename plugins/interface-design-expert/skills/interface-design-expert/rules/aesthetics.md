# Aesthetics & Visual Composition — interface-design-expert lens

**Impact: contextual — a *dial*, not a floor**, intensifying with intent (consumer / brand /
first-impression products high; safety / utility / expert tools low, where beauty collapses into
*clarity and coherence*); it never overrides the floors below, and it owns the **emphasis
budget**. Run it **after** the per-property lenses — it judges the *assembled whole*. Resolve
numbers via [`../SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md); Gestalt basis in [`foundations.md`](foundations.md).
Tags: **[evidence]** = grounded in perception research; **[craft]** = useful generative heuristic,
*not* an empirical predictor of preference — apply it, don't cite it as law.

## Principles — composition (the screen as a perceived whole)

- **Compose to one focal point. [evidence]** *(attention)* Make one element differ on a dimension
  (size, weight, color, isolation) nothing else shares — the odd-one-out is the strongest magnet.
- **Make contrast decisive, not timid. [evidence]** *(attention, fluency)* Near-miss differences
  read as mistakes; commit to clear deltas. (Craft contrast — distinct from the accessibility *floor*.)
- **Establish rhythm with repetition. [evidence]** *(grouping, fluency)* Repeat a spacing / size /
  shape interval for a scannable cadence; break it deliberately to mark a shift.
- **Treat negative space as active. [evidence]** *(grouping)* Space *is* structure, not leftover.
- **Guide the eye path. [evidence]** *(attention, convention)* Match the focal sequence to natural
  scan (reading order); let alignment and leading edges carry the eye.
- **Resolve to one coherent whole. [evidence]** *(grouping, fluency)* One intentional object with
  unambiguous figure-ground, not a pile of individually-correct parts — the bedrock under all above.
- **Balance visual weight. [craft]** *(convention)* Distribute weight (size, darkness, saturation,
  isolation) so the frame doesn't tip; asymmetry reads livelier than dead symmetry. *"Balanced =
  preferred" is a generative heuristic, not a measured law — direct tests find no reliable preference.*
- **Derive sizes from one proportion. [craft]** *(fluency)* One consistent ratio between size
  tiers makes jumps feel intentional. *Skip golden-ratio / rule-of-thirds mysticism — there's no
  reliable preference for them; a committed, consistent scale is what reads as "composed."*
- **The emphasis budget. [craft]** *(attention)* Emphasis and depth — accents, badges, motion,
  weight outliers, elevation, blur, parallax, focus-lift — spend from one finite attention
  budget, judged on the aggregate: fine per instance, in aggregate they cancel out, upstage the
  content, and tire the eye (every depth plane forces refocusing). Reserve depth for a few large
  separations, each barely noticeable, highest fidelity where attention should land.

## Principles — emotion, brand & imagery

- **Beauty is emotional, and emotion is functional. [evidence]** *(fluency, agency-trust)* Appeal
  read in the first moments shapes trust and tolerance for friction (aesthetic-usability effect;
  see foundations); invest in first-impression polish — it never substitutes for working flows.
- **Beauty is a dial tuned to intent, brand, genre, and culture. [principle]** *(convention)* No
  universal "beautiful": name the emotion you're designing for (fitness energizes; meditation
  calms) and judge *fit to it* — a maximal RPG HUD and a restrained fintech dashboard are each
  "right". Trend-chasing is a risk (trends decay); style words ("modern", "clean") are not virtues.
- **Delight is an elective move, not decoration. [principle]** *(attention)* Character at
  defining moments must never obstruct the task (absence is never a defect); most delight
  *emerges from the whole* — accumulated care, forgiveness, coherence — not bolted-on whimsy.
- **Brand lives in the whole, not in a repeated logo. [principle]** *(recognition, attention)*
  Identity rides the coherent system (palette, type, shape, motion, voice), recognizable yet at
  home on its platform; don't stamp the logo — people seldom need reminding where they are.
- **Don't brand the wait. [principle]** *(timing, agency-trust)* A loading placeholder exists to
  make startup feel fast; a splash, ad, or "About" there makes the wait feel longer and reads as
  dishonest. Place a wanted splash deliberately (e.g. onboarding), glanceable. (Mechanics: Interaction.)
- **Imagery should let people see themselves. [principle]** *(convention)* Portray a range of
  human characteristics, occupations, and settings; avoid stereotyped roles and out-of-touch affluence.

## The floors it must never breach

Aesthetics cannot buy its way below a floor — **no aesthetic justifies a breach**: legibility,
contrast, visible focus, target sizes, reduced-motion, and the **performance budget** hold no
matter how good it looks ("minimal/clean" never excuses gray-on-gray text or a hero image that
blows the performance budget). Beauty **masks problems**: judge appeal *separately from and
after* usability, and **evidence** beauty claims (desirability testing) rather than assert them.

## Checks beyond the principles

*Weight per [SKILL.md → operating loop](../SKILL.md): floors and budget overdrafts are defects regardless of intent; dials are fit-to-thesis; an elective move's absence is never a finding.*

- **Squint test:** is the single focal point findable in about a second, with one thing leading?
- **Count the emphasis spenders** on one screen — accents, badges, motion, weight outliers, depth
  cues; more than about one clear leader means the budget is overdrawn. Cite the worst spenders.
- **Anti-masking order:** judge usability *before* appeal; only then ask whether a floor is being
  traded for looks or the polish is dressing a dark pattern.
- **Tone vs. thesis:** verify visual tone against the stated intent / brand / genre, not a trend.
